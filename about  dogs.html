About Pariah Dogs

Pariah dogs rely on humans usually for their survival as well as shelter. Stray dogs are often very misunderstood, be it for their looks, their health, or even for their demeanour. Their appearance is often due to the harsh circumstances they live in and also due to the unhygienic food they eat. Irrespective of all the odds, they manage to stay resilient than most pure-bred dogs. An INDog is generally a cheerful soul. They are a highly social breed since their usual day-to-day life in India would involve interacting with a variety of other dogs and people. They enjoy being around people and dogs, which they consider to be in their family group. In the instances where they have a specific owner, they have been known to develop a strong bond of loyalty and preference to them.

Do's and Dont's

Do's

  • Stick to simple boiled foods like rice, potatoes and eggs.
  • Get vaccinations, sterilization & medical care.
  • Educate others to help you.
  • ADOPT them !.

Don't-s

  • Parle-G or any high sugar level biscuits are not good for dogs.
  • Do not hurt of harm them with stones or sticks
  • Don’t put colors on Holi.
  • Don’t burst crackers around them.

Reasons to adopt Pariah Dogs

  • Indian street dogs (Indian pariah dogs) are extremely alert & social.
  • Desi dogs have incredible Immunity.
  • Indian pariah dogs have minimal grooming needs.
  • Indian pariah dogs are extremely adaptable.
  • Stray dogs are easily trainable.
